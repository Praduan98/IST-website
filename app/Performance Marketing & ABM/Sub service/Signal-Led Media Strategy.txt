OUR APPROACH TO SIGNAL-LED MEDIA STRATEGY
 
Platform-to-Journey Mapping
We map each ad platform to specific stages of your buyer journey. LinkedIn for precision targeting and ABM. Google for high-intent search capture. Meta and YouTube for awareness and retargeting at lower CPM. Each platform has a role.
	 
Signal-Informed Budget Allocation
Budget allocation driven by where buyers show intent — not by even distribution across platforms. More spend on what’s generating pipeline. Less on what’s generating clicks.
	 
Audience Architecture
ICP-based audience design across all platforms. Cold, warm, hot, and ABM segments with distinct messaging, creative, and conversion goals. CRM and signal data feeding audience lists.
	 
Funnel Design
Full-funnel architecture: Bridge Ads for top-of-funnel warming, consideration content for middle-funnel, conversion campaigns for bottom-funnel, and ABM campaigns for named accounts.
	 
Cross-Platform Coordination
Unified messaging and sequencing across platforms. A buyer who sees a Bridge Ad on YouTube should see a relevant consideration ad on LinkedIn the following week. Coordinated, not random.
	 
OUR PHILOSOPHY
 
Front
Strategy Before Spend
Running ads without a media strategy is like driving without a map.
Platform selection, budget allocation, and funnel design should be deliberate decisions, not defaults.
Back
Most companies start running ads on LinkedIn because someone told them to. Then they add Google because search seems important. Then Meta because it’s cheap. None of it is connected. A media strategy designs the entire system first — which platform, for which audience, at which stage, with which budget.
Front
Signals Drive Allocation
Budget should follow intent, not equal distribution.
When signals tell you where buyers are active, every dollar goes where it has the highest conversion probability.
Back
If your ICP is actively searching on Google but barely engaging on Meta, your budget should reflect that. Signal-led media strategy uses real intent data and pipeline attribution to allocate budget dynamically — shifting dollars to where buyers are showing up.
Front
Platforms Are Tools, Not Strategies
LinkedIn, Google, and Meta are channels. Your strategy is how they work together.
Cross-platform coordination turns individual campaigns into a unified buyer experience.
Back
Each platform has strengths. LinkedIn targets by job title. Google captures active search intent. Meta reaches audiences at scale. YouTube builds awareness. The strategy isn’t choosing one — it’s designing how they coordinate to move buyers through your funnel.
Front
Continuous Rebalancing
A media strategy isn’t a one-time plan. It’s a living system.
As pipeline data comes in, we rebalance platforms, audiences, and budgets to maximize revenue impact.
Back
Market conditions change. Buyer behavior shifts. Platform algorithms evolve. We review media strategy monthly against pipeline data and rebalance — shifting spend, adjusting audiences, and refining the platform mix to maximize revenue.
